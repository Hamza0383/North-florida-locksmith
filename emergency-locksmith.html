What to Expect When You Call Us for an Emergency

A lot of people have been burned by locksmith companies that quote one price on the phone and present a very different number once they arrive. Here's exactly how our process works so you know what you're getting into before you commit.

Step 1 — You Call, We Answer

Every call goes to a live dispatcher on our team — day, night, weekend, holiday. We'll ask for your location, what type of property or vehicle you're locked out of, and a brief description of the situation. That information is all we need to tell you which technician is closest and give you an honest ETA.

Step 2 — We Dispatch the Nearest Technician

Your job goes to the technician who can reach you fastest, not the one who happened to answer the phone. You'll get an estimated arrival time, and if something comes up that changes it — traffic, an ongoing job running long — we'll let you know.

Step 3 — Quote First, Then We Work

When our technician arrives, they'll assess the situation and give you a clear, specific price before any work begins. That number doesn't change once we start. If the situation turns out to be more complex than described on the phone — a damaged lock, a stuck cylinder that requires additional work — we tell you what changed and what it means for the price. Your approval, then we proceed.

Step 4 — Fast, Damage-Free Entry

We use the least invasive method that gets the job done cleanly. For most residential and automotive lockouts, that means picking or shimming the lock — methods that leave no marks on your door, frame, or vehicle trim when done correctly. Drilling is a last resort and only used when no other approach is viable.

Step 5 — Everything Works Before We Leave

We test the lock, key, and door hardware before we pack up. If your lock was already showing wear before the lockout happened, we'll point it out and tell you what it means for your security going forward. You decide whether to address it then or later — we don't pressure either way.

Emergency Home Lockouts

Getting locked out of your own house is one of those situations that feels embarrassing and stressful at the same time. It's also one of the most common calls we get, and one of the faster jobs we do.

Whether you've lost your keys, locked them inside, snapped a key off in the deadbolt, or are dealing with a lock that's jammed or seized — we've handled every variation of this scenario. North Florida's coastal humidity is genuinely hard on lock cylinders over time, particularly on properties in Ponte Vedra Beach, Fernandina Beach, and the Jacksonville Beaches communities where salt air accelerates corrosion inside pin tumbler locks. If your lock has been sticking seasonally, a lockout may be the point where it finally gives up entirely — and we carry replacement hardware on every vehicle for exactly this reason.

Common residential emergency calls we handle:

  • Locked out of home — keys inside, keys lost, or keys stolen
  • Broken key stuck in a deadbolt or knob lock
  • Lock jammed or frozen due to humidity, corrosion, or mechanical failure
  • Emergency lock replacement after a break-in attempt
  • Rekeying after a security concern — lost key, tenant change, or relationship change
  • Smart lock failure — dead battery, app malfunction, or frozen keypad

What to do while you wait: Stay somewhere safe and well-lit if it's nighttime. Have your ID ready — we verify identity before entering any residence. If you have a garage door opener in your car, note that we can't verify property ownership through that alone and will still need a photo ID or utility bill with your address.

A real example: A family arrived home to their Jacksonville residence at 11:30 PM after returning from a trip to find their house key had snapped off inside the deadbolt — tired kids, luggage, and no way in. Our technician reached them within 20 minutes, extracted the broken key fragment cleanly using a spiral extractor, verified the cylinder hadn't been damaged in the process, and cut two new keys from the existing lock profile. The whole job took under an hour from the time they called.

Emergency Business Lockouts & Commercial Security Failures

A locked-out business isn't just an inconvenience — it's lost revenue, frustrated employees or customers, and a security exposure if the cause was a break-in attempt rather than a simple malfunction. We respond to commercial emergencies throughout North Florida with the same urgency as residential lockouts.

Getting You Back In Without Damaging Your Property

Commercial locks — particularly Grade 1 deadbolts, mortise locks, and lever sets on office and retail spaces — require different tools and techniques than residential hardware. We carry commercial-grade picks, bypass tools, and direct-drive equipment for high-security cylinders. Non-destructive entry is almost always possible with the right equipment and experience.

When a Break-In Has Already Occurred

If you've arrived to find evidence of a forced entry, don't touch the lock hardware before law enforcement documents the scene. Once they've cleared the property, call us for emergency lock replacement. We carry commercial-grade deadbolts, Grade 1 lever hardware, and reinforced strike plates in our vehicles for same-day installation.

Master Key System Emergencies

A failed or compromised master key system can create a significant access control problem for businesses managing multiple spaces. If a master key has been lost or stolen, the right response is to rekey the entire affected keyway — not just the locks that key was known to open. We can assess which cylinders need to be addressed, cut new keys at the appropriate hierarchy levels, and restore your access system the same day in most cases.

Access Control System Malfunctions

Electronic access control systems — keypad entry, card readers, credential-based systems — can fail due to power issues, hardware faults, or programming errors. We can diagnose the failure, implement a temporary mechanical bypass if the system includes one, and help coordinate a permanent fix without leaving your business exposed overnight.

A real example: A retail manager in Orange Park arrived at 6 AM to find their electronic entry system completely unresponsive — no power, no keypad response, no backup. Staff were arriving in 30 minutes before a busy holiday shopping day. Our technician reached them in 25 minutes, located a mechanical bypass cylinder the original installer had retained in the door hardware, got the store open on time, and returned two days later during off-hours to install a new access control panel without any disruption to operations.

Understanding Your Car Key Type — Why It Matters

Not all car keys are the same, and that directly affects how quickly and at what cost we can resolve your emergency. A standard mechanical key takes minutes to cut. A transponder key — which contains a microchip that must be matched to your vehicle's immobilizer system — takes longer and requires EEPROM or OBD-II programming equipment. Push-to-start proximity keys are the most involved, particularly on newer luxury vehicles where the security protocol is more complex. When you call, giving us the year, make, and model helps us tell you exactly what to expect before we dispatch anyone.

What to have ready when you call for a car lockout:

  • Your exact location — address, intersection, or a nearby business name
  • Vehicle year, make, model, and color
  • Whether your keys are locked inside or lost completely
  • Photo ID and vehicle registration or insurance card

Our technician reached them in 15 minutes from a nearby position, opened the vehicle using a long-reach tool with no damage to the door seal, and had the family back on their feet in under 20 minutes.

The Locksmith Scam Problem in Florida — What Every Caller Should Know

This section doesn't exist on most locksmith websites because most companies don't want to bring it up. We think that's the wrong approach.

Florida has a documented and ongoing problem with fraudulent locksmith operations. These businesses place local-looking ads with phone numbers that route to out-of-state call centers. They quote $15–$35 on the phone to secure the job, then present bills of $200–$500 or more once they're at your door and you're already in a vulnerable position. Some specifically target tourist areas — St. Augustine, Jacksonville Beach, and Amelia Island are all markets where these scams operate.

Red flags to watch for:

A quote that sounds too low to be real. A legitimate locksmith cannot profitably respond to a lockout for $15. That number is designed to get you off the phone, not to reflect what you'll actually pay.

No real business name. Scam operations typically answer with something vague like "locksmith service" or "24/7 locksmith" rather than a real company name you can look up.

No license number when you ask. Florida requires locksmiths to hold an active DBPR license. Any legitimate operation can give you their license number without hesitation. If they stall, change the subject, or don't have one, hang up.

Drilling as the first solution. Drilling a lock is a last resort that a skilled locksmith uses only when no other method works. Some scam operations drill locks by default — it justifies charging for both the labor and a forced lock replacement.

How to verify any locksmith in 60 seconds: Visit myfloridalicense.com, click "Verify a License," and enter the business name or license number. It's public information and takes less than a minute.

Frequently Asked Questions — Emergency Locksmith Services

How quickly can you reach me in North Florida?

Most of Jacksonville and the immediate metro: 15 to 30 minutes. Orange Park, Ponte Vedra Beach: similar. St. Augustine, Fernandina Beach, Yulee: 30 to 45 minutes under normal conditions. We give you a specific estimate when you call based on where our nearest technician is positioned at that moment.

What lock types can you open in an emergency?

All standard residential and commercial lock types — pin tumbler deadbolts, knob and lever hardware, mortise locks, rim locks, sliding door hardware, electronic keypads, and high-security cylinders from Schlage, Kwikset, Medeco, Mul-T-Lock, and Baldwin. For automotive, we cover all domestic and foreign passenger vehicles, most motorcycles, and many recreational and marine applications.

How do you verify I'm the rightful owner before letting me in?

For homes, a government-issued ID matching your address is standard. If your ID doesn't show your current address, a utility bill or lease agreement works. For vehicles, we need registration or insurance documents in your name. For commercial properties, we verify business ownership or authorization. This protects you as much as it protects us.

Can you make a new car key on-site?

For most vehicles, yes. Our units carry key-cutting equipment and OBD-II and EEPROM programming tools for transponder keys, remote head keys, and many push-to-start proximity keys. Some newer luxury and European vehicles with proprietary security architectures may require additional steps — we'll tell you exactly what's possible for your specific vehicle when you call.

What if I'm locked out and it's not safe to wait outside?

Tell us immediately when you call. We prioritize calls with safety concerns and will guide you to the safest place to wait — whether that's a nearby open business, inside a running vehicle, or with a neighbor. If there's any immediate danger, call 911 first. We can coordinate with law enforcement once you're safe.

Do you charge more for late-night or weekend calls?

Yes, and we'll tell you that upfront as part of the quote before we dispatch anyone. After-hours rates are real and we don't hide them. What we don't do is quote standard rates on the phone and then apply after-hours charges once we've already arrived.
