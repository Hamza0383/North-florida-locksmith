Mortise Locks — What They Are and How We Work With Them

The majority of St. Augustine's oldest residential properties — particularly in the National Historic Landmark District, Lincolnville, and the older blocks of Davis Shores — have mortise lock hardware rather than the cylindrical deadbolts found in most modern homes. Understanding the difference matters when you're calling a locksmith.

A mortise lock is a large, rectangular mechanism installed within a mortised (cut-out) pocket in the door edge. It integrates the latch, deadbolt, and often a cylinder for keyed entry into a single body that's concealed within the door. The keyway profile, the cylinder mounting, and the internal mechanics are all different from a modern cylindrical deadbolt. Most mortise cylinders use a different key profile than modern keys — some use oval-profile keys, some use older Corbin or Schlage early-series keyways, and some use proprietary profiles from manufacturers that no longer exist under that name.

What this means practically: lockout entry on a mortise lock requires different techniques than a modern deadbolt. Rekeying a mortise cylinder requires the appropriate replacement pin kit for that specific cylinder series — not every locksmith carries these. And when a mortise lock mechanism fails — a common issue in locks that are 60, 70, or 80 years old — sourcing a period-appropriate replacement or adapting a compatible modern cylinder requires knowledge of what that original lock was.

Our technicians who work in the Historic District and Lincolnville carry mortise-specific tools and a selection of mortise cylinders from the major period manufacturers. For historic properties on the local or National Register, we're also aware of the preservation guidelines that govern what hardware changes are appropriate on registered structures.

Historic St. Augustine residential properties commonly have original or period-replacement hardware from Corbin (now Corbin Russwin, still an active commercial manufacturer), Reading Hardware, early Schlage and Yale mortise series, and various regional manufacturers whose locks appear in Florida's historic housing stock. New Homeowner Service — St. Augustine's Active Resale Market

St. Augustine and St. Johns County consistently rank among Florida's most active residential real estate markets. With high transaction volume comes a real key control issue: by the time you close on a house in this market, the key history may include the previous owners, their family members, multiple showing agents, a home inspector, contractors who completed pre-sale repairs, and anyone those parties may have shared a key with.

Rekeying on move-in is straightforward and inexpensive. We can match all exterior locks to a single key in a single visit. For historic properties with mortise locks, we verify the cylinders are in serviceable condition before rekeying — an old mortise cylinder that's worn internally isn't worth rekeying; it needs cylinder replacement within the existing mortise body.

Vacation Rental and Short-Term Rental Locksmith Services

Smart Lock Configuration for Guest Management

The practical solution for most vacation rental properties in St. Augustine is a Wi-Fi-enabled smart deadbolt that allows remote code management. The workflow: when a booking is confirmed, a guest-specific code is created with a start time at check-in and an expiration at checkout. The code works during that window and becomes inactive automatically. The owner never needs to be present for key handoff and never needs to track down keys at checkout.

We install and configure these systems with the full guest access workflow tested before we leave — code creation, expiration, and manual override all verified. We also configure the separate access code structure for cleaning crews (scheduled access windows tied to their expected service times) and maintenance vendors (temporary single-use or time-limited codes).

Smart lock models we install and recommend for St. Augustine rental properties: Schlage Encode (Wi-Fi, no hub required, strong code management app), Kwikset Halo (similar capability, slightly lower price point), Yale Assure Wi-Fi, and Ultraloq U-Bolt Pro for properties where backup key entry is a priority.

For owners managing multiple rental units, we can integrate with RemoteLock or Seam API to automate code generation from your booking platform — Airbnb, VRBO, or direct booking systems — so the entire guest access cycle runs without manual intervention.

Rekeying Between Long-Term Tenants

For rental properties with longer-term tenants, rekeying the property at the end of each tenancy is standard practice. We handle these on short notice — same-day or next-morning service when a tenant has vacated and the property needs to turn over quickly.

Commercial Locksmith Services in St. Augustine

The Historic District Business Corridor

St. George Street and the commercial blocks around the Castillo de San Marcos, the Lightner Museum, and Flagler College form one of Florida's densest tourist commercial concentrations. Businesses in this corridor have specific security considerations: high foot traffic past storefront doors, seasonal staff with key access, high employee turnover in the hospitality sector, and historic building constraints that affect what hardware modifications are possible.

For retail and hospitality businesses on St. George Street and adjacent blocks, restricted keyway commercial cylinders are a practical security investment. Standard commercial keyways can be duplicated at any hardware store kiosk — meaning an employee who leaves with a key can have copies made before you can get the locks rekeyed. Restricted keyway cylinders from Medeco, Mul-T-Lock, or ASSA Abloy use patented key profiles that can only be cut by authorized dealers, requiring written authorization from the account holder. Key control becomes meaningful rather than theoretical.

Flagler College and Institutional Properties

Flagler College's campus, concentrated in and around the historic Ponce de León Hotel building and adjacent structures, has complex access control requirements across a mix of historic and modern construction. We work with institutional clients throughout the St. Augustine area on master key system design, access control installation, and commercial hardware service.

St. Augustine Shores and County Commercial Districts

The commercial properties along US-1 south of the historic core, in St. Augustine Shores, and throughout the county's suburban commercial areas have standard commercial hardware requirements — Grade 1 deadbolt hardware, commercial mortise locks on high-traffic entry doors, panic hardware on egress doors, and access control for multi-employee facilities. We serve these properties with the same commercial hardware knowledge we bring to historic district work.
